# Recommended Standardized Tests & Scales

> These are clinically validated questionnaires you can take online.
> Record your score and date. Most take 2-5 minutes each.
>
> **Raw data requirement:** Every test below includes a link where the full questions + your answers (not just a summary score) can be downloaded or saved. For paper-based instruments, use the fillable PDF so you retain the complete document.

## Priority (do these first)

### PHQ-9 — Depression
- **Takes:** 2 min
- **Why:** Quantifies current depression severity. Anchors depression PRS if you have one.
- **Where:** Fill out the [PHQ-9 fillable PDF](https://med.stanford.edu/fastlab/research/imapp/msrs/_jcr_content/main/accordion/accordion_content3/tab_content/items/tab4/inner0/accordion/accordion_content4/tab_content1/items/tab9/inner0/download_256324296/file.res/PHQ9%20id%20date.pdf) — public domain, save the filled PDF as your raw data.
- **Alt:** [screening.mhanational.org](https://screening.mhanational.org/screening-tools/depression/) — online, shows all Q&A on results page (screenshot or print-to-PDF).
- **Score interpretation:** 0-4 minimal, 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-19 moderately severe, 20-27 severe

### GAD-7 — Anxiety
- **Takes:** 2 min
- **Why:** Quantifies current anxiety.
- **Where:** Fill out the [GAD-7 fillable PDF](https://med.stanford.edu/fastlab/research/imapp/msrs/_jcr_content/main/accordion/accordion_content3/tab_content/items/tab4/inner0/accordion/accordion_content4/tab_content1/items/tab8/inner0/download_256324296/file.res/GAD7%20id%20date.pdf) — public domain, save the filled PDF.
- **Alt:** [screening.mhanational.org](https://screening.mhanational.org/screening-tools/anxiety/) — online version with full Q&A on results page.
- **Score interpretation:** 0-4 minimal, 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-21 severe

### ISI — Insomnia Severity Index
- **Takes:** 2 min
- **Why:** Standardized insomnia measure.
- **Where:** [mysleepwell.ca ISI PDF](https://mysleepwell.ca/cbti/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/ISI.pdf) — fill out and save. Or use the [Sleepstation online ISI](https://www.sleepstation.org.uk/insomnia-test/) (screenshot results).
- **Score interpretation:** 0-7 none, 8-14 subthreshold, 15-21 moderate, 22-28 severe

### ESS — Epworth Sleepiness Scale
- **Takes:** 2 min
- **Why:** Daytime sleepiness. Differentiates "tired but wired" (insomnia) from "falling asleep during activities" (hypersomnia/narcolepsy/sleep apnea).
- **Where:** [epworthsleepinessscale.com](https://epworthsleepinessscale.com/about-the-ess/) — official site with the 8 questions and scoring. Print-to-PDF to retain raw data.
- **Score interpretation:** 0-10 normal, 11-14 mild excessive, 15-17 moderate, 18-24 severe

### COMPASS-31 — Autonomic Symptom Profile
- **Takes:** 10-15 min
- **Why:** Comprehensive autonomic symptom questionnaire. Critical for any dysautonomia workup.
- **Where:** [Bateman Horne Center COMPASS-31 PDF](https://bfrlab.com/Uploads/Compass/COMPASS-31_with_Scoring.pdf) — downloadable PDF with all 31 questions and built-in scoring sheet. Fill out and save.
- **Score interpretation:** Higher = more autonomic dysfunction. >20 suggests significant dysautonomia.

## Secondary (do if energy permits)

### MDQ — Mood Disorder Questionnaire
- **Takes:** 5 min
- **Why:** Screens for bipolar spectrum.
- **Where:** [screening.mhanational.org](https://screening.mhanational.org/screening-tools/bipolar/) — shows all Q&A on results page. Print-to-PDF.
- **Alt:** [DBSAlliance MDQ PDF](https://www.dbsalliance.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/MDQ.pdf) — fillable.
- **Score interpretation:** Score ≥7, with symptoms concurrent and causing ≥moderate problems = positive screen

### HCL-32 — Hypomania Checklist
- **Takes:** 5 min
- **Why:** More sensitive than MDQ for hypomania/soft bipolar. Focuses on "high" periods.
- **Where:** Paper instrument — search "HCL-32 PDF" for the questionnaire. Fill out and save PDF.
- **Score interpretation:** ≥14 positive screen for bipolar spectrum

### ASRS v1.1 — Adult ADHD
- **Takes:** 3 min
- **Why:** ADHD symptoms can mimic or co-occur with mood disorders and sleep deprivation.
- **Where:** [Add.org ASRS PDF](https://add.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/adhd-questionnaire-ASRS111.pdf) — official WHO 6-item screener, fillable PDF. Or [screening.mhanational.org](https://screening.mhanational.org/screening-tools/adhd/) for online version.
- **Score interpretation:** Part A ≥4 shaded = positive screen

### PCL-5 — PTSD Checklist
- **Takes:** 5 min
- **Why:** If cortisol diurnal profile is abnormal (flattened, absent CAR), that pattern is consistent with PTSD/chronic trauma.
- **Where:** [VA.gov PCL-5 PDF](https://www.ptsd.va.gov/professional/assessment/documents/PCL5_Standard_form.PDF) — official NCPTSD version, 20 items, fill out and save.
- **Score interpretation:** ≥31 probable PTSD

### DES-II — Dissociative Experiences Scale
- **Takes:** 5 min
- **Why:** Gold standard dissociation measure, if dissociative symptoms are present.
- **Where:** Paper instrument — [DES-II PDF](https://traumadissociation.com/des) has the full 28-item scale. Fill out, compute mean, save PDF.
- **Score interpretation:** Mean >30 = significant dissociation

### Beighton Score — Joint Hypermobility
- **Takes:** 2 min (physical, not online)
- **Why:** EDS/hypermobility is a common dysautonomia comorbidity.
- **Test:** Can you: touch floor with palms flat (knees straight)? Hyperextend elbows >10°? Hyperextend knees >10°? Bend thumbs to wrist? Bend pinky >90°?
- **Score:** 1 point per side, max 9. ≥5 = generalized joint hypermobility.
- **Raw data:** Record each of the 9 maneuvers (L/R where applicable) as pass/fail. Take photos of each test position.

### ME/CFS Diagnostic Criteria (IOM 2015)
- **Not a test — a checklist:**
  1. Substantial reduction in activity lasting >6 months
  2. Post-exertional malaise (symptoms worsen 12-48h after exertion)
  3. Unrefreshing sleep
  4. Plus: cognitive impairment AND/OR orthostatic intolerance
- **Why:** If you have fatigue + sleep issues + autonomic symptoms, this differential is worth considering.

## Personality Assessments

### IPIP-NEO-120 — Big Five
- **Takes:** 20 min
- **Why:** Gold standard personality assessment. Free, open source, massive normative data. Maps openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, neuroticism + 30 facets.
- **Where:** [bigfive-test.com](https://bigfive-test.com) — open source, gives shareable results URL with full facet breakdown.
- **Raw data:** Results URL contains your full response profile.

### TCI-R — Cloninger Temperament & Character
- **Takes:** 20 min
- **Why:** Maps onto neurotransmitter systems (novelty seeking = dopamine, harm avoidance = serotonin). Pairs directly with COMT and PGx data. Most genomics-relevant personality instrument.
- **Where:** [psytests.org TCI](https://psytests.org/temperament/tcien.html) — free IPIP-equivalent TCI. Save results page as PDF.

## Optional / Tracking

### Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI)
- **Takes:** 5 min
- **Why:** More comprehensive than ISI. Covers sleep quality, latency, duration, efficiency, disturbances.
- **Where:** [PSQI PDF](https://www.sleep.pitt.edu/instruments/) — original source from University of Pittsburgh.

### FSS — Fatigue Severity Scale
- **Takes:** 2 min
- **Why:** Quantifies fatigue impact on daily function.
- **Where:** 9-item Likert scale — [FSS PDF available from various clinical sources](https://www.sralab.org/rehabilitation-measures/fatigue-severity-scale).

### SF-36 — Quality of Life
- **Takes:** 10 min
- **Why:** General health-related quality of life. Useful baseline for tracking interventions.
- **Where:** [rand.org SF-36](https://www.rand.org/health-care/surveys_tools/mos/36-item-short-form.html) — original RAND version, free for non-commercial use.
