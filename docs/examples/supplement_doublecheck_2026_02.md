### COI problem (unchanged)
- Nearly all positive human trials are funded by Amazentis/Timeline (Mitopure manufacturer)
- Timeline itself raised alarm about Amazon UA supplements (NutraIngredients Aug 2023) — genuine quality concern but also competitive self-interest
- The independent systematic reviews (Watts 2025, Hodzic Kuerecab 2024) are from academic groups with no industry ties
- Brad Stanfield conspicuously does NOT take UA

### Disconfirmation
- **Watts systematic review:** "Insufficient evidence to support the use of UA to improve muscle function in any population at present" — the strongest independent academic assessment
- Only 3 RCTs qualified for systematic review despite growing literature
- No negative/null published trials found, but sample sizes are tiny (most n=30-50)
- Ageing Res Rev systematic review: UA did NOT affect mitochondrial ATP production, biogenesis, or dynamics — key claimed mechanisms showed no effect in humans

### Mitopure Decision
The "parked" status remains reasonable. Evidence marginally stronger but COI unchanged. Watch ISRCTN47159002 results (~2027). Timeline Mitopure is the only quality-validated brand ($100-125/mo).

### Updated assessment
The "COSMOS failed" headline is misleading — the Ottaviani 2025 adherence reanalysis explains why the ITT primary endpoint was non-significant (background diet contamination + non-compliance). Multiple independent 2025-2026 RCTs confirm vascular endothelial benefits. Don't expect acute cognitive boosts (null in young adults). Long-term cardiovascular protection is the real value proposition.

## 3. Magic Mind FREE — Skip

### Product profile
- **Proprietary blend** (1,925mg): Bacopa Monnieri, Lion's Mane, Ashwagandha, Citicoline (Cognizin), Cordyceps, Rhodiola Rosea, L-Theanine, Turmeric
- Vitamins: C 100mg, D3 100mcg (500% DV), B2 1mg, B3 8mg, B12 2.4mcg
- 0mg caffeine, 0g sugar
- Cost: ~$2.50/shot (subscription) to ~$5.10/shot (one-time) = $75-150/month

### Critical problems
1. **Proprietary blend** — individual ingredient doses not disclosed. 8 ingredients sharing 1,925mg = ~240mg average. Clinical thresholds: Ashwagandha 300-600mg, Bacopa 300mg, Rhodiola 200-600mg, Citicoline 250-500mg. Most likely sub-clinical.
2. **Confirmed underdosing** — Innerbody review reported Bacopa ~125mg (clinical: 300mg), Citicoline ~125mg (clinical: 250-500mg). NootropicsExpert: "most of them seem a little underdosed."
3. **No independent testing verification** — company claims third-party testing, provides zero proof (NootropicsExpert)
4. **No peer-reviewed efficacy study** — their study was not placebo-controlled or peer-reviewed
5. **Cost-ineffective** — $75-150/month for sub-clinical doses. Individual ingredients at clinical doses: ~$30/month
6. **Mixed-to-negative consumer reviews** — BBB complaints, some review sites flagged as scam-adjacent. 78% recommend on Thingtesting but many received free product.

### Better alternative (~$30/month)
- Ashwagandha KSM-66 600mg ($12/mo)
- Citicoline (Cognizin) 500mg ($15/mo)
- L-Theanine 200mg ($8/mo — or just drink matcha)

## 4. Magic Mind Sleep — Skip

### Product profile
- **11 ingredients** (no individual doses): Ashwagandha, Kava, Chamomile, GABA, 5-HTP, L-Theanine, Valerian, Passionflower, Lemon Balm, Lavender, Magnesium Glycinate
- Melatonin-free, non-habit-forming
- Cost: ~$2.50-5.00/shot = $75-150/month

### Critical problems
1. **Proprietary blend** — 11 active ingredients in 2oz. Doses almost certainly sub-clinical for most. Magnesium glycinate clinical dose: 200-400mg elemental Mg — this likely contains a fraction.
2. **Oral GABA** — poor blood-brain barrier penetration. Evidence for oral GABA improving sleep is weak even at targeted doses (100-200mg PharmaGABA).
3. **Kava safety concern** — hepatotoxicity risk; restricted/banned in some countries. Unknown extract type and dose in a proprietary blend is a red flag.
4. **5-HTP interaction potential** — if combined with SSRIs/serotonergic drugs, even low-dose could theoretically contribute. Unknown dose makes risk assessment impossible.
5. **Kitchen-sink formulation** — 11 ingredients suggests marketing-driven, not science-driven. No single ingredient gets enough room to work.
6. **No peer-reviewed study** on this specific formula.

### Better alternative (~$10-20/month)
- Magnesium glycinate 400mg elemental ($10/mo) — strongest evidence-backed sleep supplement
- Optional: L-Theanine 200mg ($8/mo)
